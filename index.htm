Not an Avocado

A One-Man Medical Software Development Team

Not an Avocado is a Sydney-based software developer specialising in medical and educational software packages.

There are currently two primary packages in the works: PharQuiz, which aims become a fully-fledged revision tool for scientific and medical educational settings, and TremorSense, which aims to produce a standardised, quantified measurement scale for tremor associated with a variety of neuro- and psychological contitions.

PharQuiz

PharQuiz is currently in an early, unstable state of development. As it stands, the tool is capable of assessing knowledge of a drug's class, molecular action, clinical use, mechanism of action and adverse effects. This is going to be expanded over the coming months into a more flexible tool for educational assessment.

TremorSense

It's somewhat remarkable that there is no standard scale for quantifying tremor. At the moment, if you present to a clinic with tremor, the standard methods of assessment are visual examination by the practitioner and the "glass test," which is essentially a measure of how difficult it is for the patient to pick up and drink from a glass of water. TremorSense aims to quantify tremor intensity by a specialised algorithm that measures vibrations via the accelerometers in a smartphone (currently limited to Android).

Tremor is associated with a variety of conditions of neurological and psychiatric origin: Parkinson's disease, schizophrenia, anxiety, essential tremor, and as a side effect of medication.
